Partnership Value by Partner Type

Big Tech Platforms (Amazon, Apple, GitHub, Microsoft)

What They Care About

  • Ecosystem fit and product integration
  • Developer adoption and platform stickiness
  • Edge/hybrid cloud strategy validation
  • User trust and privacy positioning
  • Competitive differentiation

Key Emphasis

  • ARIA complements cloud services for offline/edge scenarios
  • Reference implementation for local AI on their hardware
  • Developer productivity tool that enhances their platforms
  • Privacy story aligns with their positioning

Appropriate Ask

  • Technical evaluation with engineering teams
  • Integration exploration (SDK, APIs, platform hooks)
  • Co-development partnership for specific use cases
  • OEM licensing for device integration

Hardware Partners (NVIDIA)

What They Care About

  • GPU and Jetson utilization (silicon pull-through)
  • Developer ecosystem and workload diversity
  • Reference designs that showcase hardware
  • Enterprise and defense market penetration

Key Emphasis

  • ARIA is a "hero workload" that showcases NVIDIA edge AI
  • Dual compute path (CUDA + MLX) demonstrates flexibility
  • Powerhouse AI with Jetson = turnkey defense solution
  • Developer pull-through: ARIA devs buy NVIDIA hardware

Appropriate Ask

  • Developer program partnership
  • Jetson reference design co-development
  • GPU allocation for development/testing
  • Joint marketing for enterprise/defense AI

Investors (Seed, Series A)

What They Care About

  • Market size and timing
  • Technical moat and defensibility
  • Revenue model and unit economics
  • Traction and evidence of demand
  • Team and execution capability
  • Exit path and return potential

Key Emphasis

  • $140B TAM across privacy-sensitive verticals
  • 80% gross margins (SaaS economics)
  • Technical moat: multi-agent architecture, compliance, recovery
  • Production-ready today (not vaporware)
  • Exit paths: strategic acquisition ($500M-$2B) or IPO

Appropriate Ask

  • Seed: $2M for 18 months to reach $3M ARR
  • Series A: $15-20M for scale-up
  • Clear use of funds and milestones
  • Board seat or observer rights

DoD / DARPA

What They Care About

  • Mission need and operational relevance
  • Technology Readiness Level (TRL)
  • Risk reduction and transition path
  • Compliance and security posture
  • Cost and schedule realism

Key Emphasis

  • Denied/degraded/disconnected (D3) environments
  • Self-healing autonomy (no manual intervention)
  • Pre-LLM compliance (not redaction)
  • Air-gap verification (cryptographic)
  • Clear operational scenarios (TOC, SIGINT, classified dev)
  • TRL 7-8 today, TRL 8-9 achievable in 24 months

Appropriate Ask

  • SBIR/STTR Phase I/II ($150K-$1.5M)
  • OTA prototype ($2M-$5M)
  • DARPA BAA proposal ($6M-$15M)
  • Pilot deployment with operational unit
